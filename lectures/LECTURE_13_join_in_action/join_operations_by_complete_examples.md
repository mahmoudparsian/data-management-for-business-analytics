## FULL-JOIN

	MySQL does NOT support FULL JOIN, 
	but you can simulate it using a UNION 
	of LEFT JOIN and RIGHT JOIN.

![](./images/JOIN_FULL.png)

```sql
SELECT 
    t1.id,
    t1.value1,
    t2.id AS t2_id,
    t2.value2
FROM t1
LEFT JOIN t2 ON t1.id = t2.id

UNION

SELECT
    t1.id,
    t1.value1,
    t2.id AS t2_id,
    t2.value2
FROM t2
LEFT JOIN t1 ON t2.id = t1.id;
+------+--------+-------+--------+
| id   | value1 | t2_id | value2 |
+------+--------+-------+--------+
|    1 | v1     |     1 | w2     |
|    1 | v1     |     1 | w1     |
|    1 | v2     |     1 | w2     |
|    1 | v2     |     1 | w1     |
|    2 | v3     |     2 | w5     |
|    2 | v3     |     2 | w4     |
|    2 | v3     |     2 | w3     |
```
